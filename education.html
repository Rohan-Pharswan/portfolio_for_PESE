Lecture 4: Group Discussion learning

In out fourth lecture I learnt about do's and don'ts in a group discussion

✅ Dos:

  • Enter Confidently: Start with a calm, confident greeting if possible.
  • Listen Actively: Show you value others' opinions.
  • Speak Clearly & Concisely: Be to the point, avoid rambling.
  • Support with Facts or Examples: Strengthens your argument.
  • Encourage Others: Let quieter members speak, shows leadership.
  • Maintain Eye Contact: With the group, not just the moderator.
  • Stay Calm & Polite: Even in disagreement.
  • Be a Team Player: Add value to the group, not just your own point.

❌ Don'ts:

  • Don't Interrupt Rudely: Wait for a pause or use polite cues.
  • Don't Dominate the Discussion: Let everyone contribute.
  • Don't Be Aggressive or Personal: Focus on the issue, not the person.
  • Don't Go Off-Topic: Stay relevant to the main point.
  • Don't Speak Without Thinking: Quality matters more than quantity.
  • Don't Use Jargon: Keep it simple and clear.
  • Don't Be Too Quiet: Not contributing can be seen as lack of initiative.

Movie: The Shawshank Redemption

The Shawshank Redemption The Shawshank Redemption is more than just a film—it's a moving portrayal of hope, perseverance, and inner freedom. Based on Stephen King's novella Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption, the movie tells the story of Andy Dufresne, a banker wrongly imprisoned for the murder of his wife and her lover. Through his time in Shawshank prison, Andy becomes a symbol of quiet resilience and integrity. His journey is intertwined with that of Ellis "Red" Redding, played masterfully by Morgan Freeman, whose narration adds warmth and wisdom to the story. What makes this movie stand out is its ability to capture the emotional and psychological transformation of its characters. It doesn't rely on action or spectacle—instead, it digs deep into human nature, the value of friendship, and the undying power of trust.
